# Aesthetics-KG

This project proposes a semantic formalization and large-scale knowledge graph of aesthetics, Aesthetics-KG, conceived as philosophical, conceptual and cultural categories rather than merely visual or stylistic labels. While aesthetics are widely documented in informal and community-driven resources, they lack a structured representation that captures their conceptual, symbolic, and relational dimensions. Still, they are used everyday even in recommendation systems and query systems on the
biggest online websites. Building on the Vibe ontology, an ontology of aesthetics, we use data collected from online repositories (e.g., Aesthetics Wiki), to construct a knowledge graph covering over one thousand aesthetic entities, prioritizing breadth of coverage as a first step toward semantic consolidation. Beyond formalization, the project explores the integration of knowledge graphs with large language models to extract analogical and metaphorical relations between aesthetics. This enables the identification of latent connections, hybridizations, and emergent aesthetic configurations, contributing to a richer understanding of aesthetic organization across domains such as digital culture, literature, design, and architecture. The resulting framework supports semantic querying, cultural analysis, and potential applications in areas such as AI interpretability, digital humanities, and creative industries.
